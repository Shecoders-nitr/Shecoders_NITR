About Us

The fast evolving face of technology today sets higher demands women to get more invoved in Tech.

  • Shecoders.tech is a 36-hour hackathon hosted by Shecoders, The women coding community, a student-run club at NIT Rourkela. Through the event, we encourage all individuals to learn about and become change agents for the gender gap in technology by innovating and inspiring with code.
  • We strive to educate as many people as we can about the obstacles females (trans and cis) and other minorities face in computing and hope to inspire more unified action through our hackathon. Above all, we believe that people of all genders working together is key to creating an inclusive environment of mutual respect.
  • Shecoders.tech is a female-empowerment event, but not female-only. We believe in the value of hosting events that bring together people of all genders under a common goal: to make tech an inclusive space that celebrates women (trans and cis) and other minorities. Those who do not identify as female are highly encouraged to participate in the event and join us for a weekend of innovation, camaraderie, and female-empowerment.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is a hackathon?

    A "hackathon" is an innovative event where teams work on developing tech projects in a limited period of time. Hackathons typically consist of workshops, activities, food, swag, meeting other awesome people in tech, and so much more to keep "hackers" engaged and fueled throughout the night.

  • What? Where? When?

    Shecoders.tech is a 36-hour female-empowerment hackathon and will take place Saturday, November 5th - Sunday, November 6th via Discord, Zoom, other online platforms. Due to COVID-19, Shecoders.tech will be fully virtual this 2022 season.

  • Do I need a team?

    If you are planning to demo your project to compete for a prize, you must be in a team of 2-4 people. If you don't have a team yet, no worries! We'll have team formation activities and plenty of ways for you to meet other people! There should be more than or equal to 50% female members in each team.

  • How do I become a mentor or judge?

    To become a mentor or a judge for Shecoders.tech, fill out the form listed below. For more information about the roles, check out the announcement we posted under the Announcements tab.

  • Who can attend HackViolet?

    Any student enrolled in a high school, undergraduate, or graduate program. We are not a female (trans and cis)-only event. We invite students of all genders, majors, and backgrounds. There is absolutely no experience necessary!

  • Do I need to know how to code?

    Absolutely not! We encourage participants of all skill levels to join us. We will have introductory workshops and access to mentors to teach you everything you need to know!

  • I have other questions! How do I reach out?

    Feel free to contact us at Email: shecoders@gmail.com
